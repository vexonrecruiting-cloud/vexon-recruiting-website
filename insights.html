Industry Insights

Practical observations from Canada’s skilled trades hiring market.

Short, useful notes for employers and candidates working across mining, heavy equipment, industrial maintenance, manufacturing and power engineering.

Power Engineering

Why 2nd and 3rd Class Power Engineer searches become difficult quickly

Certification alone is not always enough. Employers often require specific boiler, refrigeration, utility, process or plant operating experience. Location and shift preferences can narrow the available pool even further. Successful searches usually begin with a clear explanation of the plant, schedule, compensation and growth path.

Heavy Equipment

What experienced HETs compare before considering a move

Hourly pay matters, but experienced technicians also assess rotation, travel arrangements, camp quality, overtime structure, equipment makes, tool allowances and the balance between shop and field work. Clear details help attract serious candidates and reduce wasted interviews.

Industrial Maintenance

Why passive tradespeople need a compelling reason to respond

Many qualified millwrights and industrial electricians are already employed. A vague job description rarely creates enough interest. Strong outreach explains the work, schedule, equipment, compensation, location and why the opportunity is meaningfully better than the candidate’s current position.

Recruitment Process

Quality improves when hiring requirements are specific

Recruitment is more effective when the employer distinguishes mandatory requirements from preferences. Trade certification, exact equipment background, years of experience, schedule, travel, work authorization and compensation should be clear before sourcing begins.
